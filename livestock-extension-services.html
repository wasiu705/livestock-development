Department Mandate

The Department of Livestock Extension and Business Development (LEBD) strengthens veterinary, livestock and fisheries extension delivery, enterprise development, and value-chain facilitation across Edo State.

It serves as the facilitation arm of the Ministry, supporting the technical departments—Veterinary Services, Veterinary Public Health, Animal Husbandry Services, and Fisheries & Aquaculture—by translating their policies, programs, and technical innovations into practical field actions for farmers and cooperatives.

While regulatory and control functions remain under the technical departments as provided by the Codex Alimentarius, technical departments’ laws, and related statutes, LEBD ensures effective coordination, business promotion, and stakeholder participation to achieve the Ministry’s livestock development goals.

Departmental Roles & Responsibilities

  • Coordinate livestock and fisheries extension systems across all 18 LGAs in collaboration with technical departments
  • Translate scientific and veterinary innovations into farmer-friendly training modules and field demonstrations
  • Facilitate agribusiness incubation, cooperative formation, and value-chain promotion for improved productivity and market access
  • Provide technical, engineering, and infrastructure support for projects initiated by the technical departments
  • Promote digital extension systems for farmer registration, advisory dissemination, and market data sharing
  • Support project planning and monitoring with the Department of Planning, Research & Statistics (DPRS)
  • Strengthen stakeholder engagement, public-private partnerships, and donor collaboration
  • Assist technical departments in quality facilitation, product improvement, and compliance support
  • Build professional and vocational skills for extension officers, cooperative leaders, and small-scale producers
