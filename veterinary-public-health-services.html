Veterinary Public Health Services

Safeguarding animal and public health through disease surveillance, food safety, One Health initiatives, and strict biosecurity compliance across all production systems.

Dr. Odiaua Joseph Ayenmere

Director, Veterinary Public Health Services

Oversees the state's veterinary public health framework, ensuring rigorous food safety standards, zoonotic disease control, and One Health coordination. With extensive expertise in epidemiology and regulatory enforcement, he leads policy implementation, facility licensing, and outbreak response to protect both animal welfare and community health.

Department Mandate

To safeguard animal and public health through coordinated disease surveillance, prevention, and control; implementation of One Health and AMR programs; enforcement of biosecurity and sanitary measures; regulation of food safety and quality; and oversight of livestock identification, traceability, and facility licensing across all animal production and processing systems.

Departmental Roles & Responsibilities

  • Formulate and implement State policies on veterinary public health, food safety, zoonotic disease control, and biosecurity
  • Plan, coordinate, and evaluate animal-disease surveillance, immunization, prophylaxis, and outbreak response
  • Lead One Health, AMR/AMU surveillance, and antimicrobial-stewardship programs across animal, human, and environmental sectors
  • Regulate, register, and license all livestock and animal-product facilities including farms, hatcheries, feed mills, abattoirs, slaughter slabs, meat shops, milk and egg centers, cold rooms, apiaries, and livestock markets
  • Formulate and enforce sanitary and structural standards for all animal facilities
  • Certify animal-origin products through food-safety, hygiene, and quality-assurance programs
  • Implement and monitor biosecurity compliance at farms, markets, abattoirs, and during animal movement
  • Operate and maintain the Livestock Identification and Traceability System (LITS) and livestock control posts
  • Conduct vector and pest surveillance, environmental sanitation, and control programs
  • Coordinate One Health surveillance and information sharing with Ministries of Health and Environment
  • Provide authoritative technical direction on all matters of veterinary public health and disease surveillance within the State

Specialized Divisions

Core operational units driving surveillance, certification, facility regulation, and traceability.

Disease Control & Epidemiology Division

  • Conduct veterinary epidemiological surveillance and outbreak investigation
  • Coordinate data collection, analysis, and reporting of animal disease trends
  • Lead epidemic preparedness and response across zoonotic and transboundary diseases (e.g., HPAI, ASF, anthrax, rabies)
  • Maintain the state animal disease information system and link to national databases
  • Implement One Health coordination with human health, environment, and agriculture sectors
  • Produce periodic animal health bulletins and situation reports
  • Ensure herd immunity by organizing and implementing mass vaccination programs
  • Promote biosecurity in farms and enforce quarantine and culling in cases of outbreaks

Certification & Quality Control Division

  • Issue veterinary health certificates for the movement, export, and import of animal products
  • Ensure proper hygiene, handling, and processing of animal products
  • Monitor veterinary drugs, vaccines, and biologicals to confirm safety, potency, and quality
  • Implement and enforce veterinary public health regulations and food safety laws
  • Ensure compliance with Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP) and HACCP principles
  • Provide official veterinary certification documents required for trade

Animal Traceability & Control Post Division

  • Establish and manage a Livestock Identification and Traceability System (LITS) for animal movement control
  • Operate and supervise livestock control posts, quarantine stations, and border checkpoints
  • Register herds and individual animals through tagging, branding, or RFID systems
  • Issue and verify livestock movement permits (LMPs) and ensure compliance with movement restrictions
  • Integrate movement data into surveillance and disease response systems
  • Collaborate with security, transport, and customs agencies to prevent illegal livestock movement

Vector / Pest Control Division

  • Develop and implement integrated vector and pest management programs to control disease carriers
  • Conduct surveillance for arthropods, rodents, and other vectors affecting animal health
  • Monitor seasonal trends and outbreaks of vector-borne diseases
  • Study vector ecology, breeding habits, and transmission patterns to inform control strategies
  • Design integrated vector management strategies using chemical, biological, and environmental methods
  • Supervise use of veterinary-approved pesticides and disinfectants
  • Evaluate control method effectiveness and develop improved techniques
  • Coordinate environmental sanitation around livestock premises and markets
  • Collaborate with Environmental Health and Agriculture agencies on pest control campaigns

Abattoir Development & Food Safety Hygiene Division

Oversees the design, regulation, and monitoring of abattoirs, as well as the implementation of food safety and hygiene standards to protect both animal and public health. Ensures facilities meet structural, sanitary, and operational benchmarks for safe meat processing and distribution.
