Animal Husbandry Services

Driving sustainable livestock production, genetic improvement, and value chain commercialization for economic empowerment and food security.

Mrs. Agho Loveth. E

Director, Animal Husbandry Services

Leads the strategic development of Edo State's livestock production systems, focusing on genetic improvement, commercialization of value chains, and sustainable pastoralist integration. With deep expertise in animal Husbandry services, she coordinates breeding programs, ranch development, and vocational skill building to maximize productivity and farmer livelihoods.

Contact Department

Department Mandate

  • To improve livestock production and productivity across the state
  • To strengthen the commercialization of livestock value chains where the State has a comparative advantage
  • To improve livestock productivity and commercialization of selected value chains and strengthen the state’s capacity to respond to eligible crises or emergencies

Core Departmental Roles & Responsibilities

  • Formulation of policies on Livestock Resources, production, conservation, and livestock projects
  • Facilitating the establishment of Grazing Reserves and Stock Route Development
  • Promoting the development of ranches and livestock markets
  • Promoting economic empowerment of pastoralists and other Livestock farmers
  • Operating breeding and conservation centers for genetic stock improvement
  • Manpower and Vocational Skill Development in Animal Husbandry
  • Coordination of all livestock programmes and projects across divisions

Key Responsibilities by Division

Specialized units driving monogastric, ruminant, and feed/product development initiatives.

Monogastric & Micro Livestock Division

  • Promote manpower and vocational skills in monogastric & micro livestock production
  • Research, source and document data to support production systems
  • Provide advisory and support services to farmers on production challenges
  • Regulate hatchery operations using NIAS standards
  • Monitor and control use of unwholesome substances like unapproved hormones
  • Coordinate genetic improvement, AI campaigns, and record-keeping programs
  • Manage development of pigs, poultry, rabbits with technical support on breeding, housing & feeding
  • Support propagation of micro-livestock (snails, grasscutters, apiculture) & guide exotic animal care

Ruminants Division

  • Promote manpower and vocational skills in ruminant animal production
  • Research, source and document data to support ruminant production
  • Provide advisory and support services to farmers
  • Facilitate conflict resolution for farmers-herders clashes using Alternative Dispute Resolution
  • Enhance cattle, sheep, and goats through breed improvement initiatives
  • Establish & manage ranches, ensure best practices in range management & animal welfare
  • Coordinate genetic improvement, AI campaigns, and record-keeping
  • Implement policies & technologies for issuing identification tags & maintaining ruminant records
  • Monitor and control use of unapproved hormones in ruminant production

Animal Produce, Products Devt & Feed Processing

  • Ensure quality control of feedstuff & standardization of finished feeds per recommended nutritional levels
  • Monitor, regulate and license local feed mills
  • Regulate feed mill operations, animal handling & meat processing per NIAS standards
  • Facilitate establishment of standard storage facilities in strategic areas for processing & marketing
  • Promote innovation & value addition in livestock products (meat, egg, milk, hides) & support quality improvement
  • Develop & enforce standard operational guidelines for animal products processing & marketing
  • Provide capacity building on animal products handling for butchers & vendors in collaboration with Animal Health
  • Facilitate private sector participation in feed & animal products processing
  • Research, promote, & manage feed/fodder production (silage, hay, alternative feeds) to optimize nutrition
