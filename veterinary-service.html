Veterinary Services Department

Delivering science-based animal healthcare, disease control, and welfare standards across all livestock and companion species in Edo State.

Dr. Haruna

Director, Veterinary Services

Leads the state's veterinary healthcare delivery system, ensuring professional standards, disease surveillance, and animal welfare across all species. With extensive clinical and administrative expertise, he oversees veterinary divisions, outbreak response protocols, and strategic partnerships to safeguard livestock productivity and public health.

Contact Department

Department Core Mandate

To deliver curative, reproductive, diagnostic, regulatory, and wildlife-related veterinary services; uphold professional standards; and ensure animal welfare, health, and productivity through science-based veterinary interventions.

Veterinary Clinical Services Division

Provides field and clinical veterinary healthcare, disease diagnosis, treatment, and emergency response. Supervises clinics, mobile health units, and ambulatory services while maintaining clinical records for public health surveillance.

Small Animal Unit

  • Provide specialized care to pets
  • Provide medical treatment, managing infectious, metabolic and genetic diseases and stabilizing emergency cases
  • Vaccination of pets against common diseases e.g. rabies
  • Perform routine surgeries like spaying, neutering and other surgical procedures
  • Provides deworming and parasite control

Aquatic Unit

  • Carries out vaccination programs for fishes
  • Advises on quarantine and disinfection of new or infected stocks
  • Performs clinical examination, prescribes and supervises medicated feed or bath treatment
  • Conducts post-mortem to determine causes of mortality
  • Investigates disease outbreaks and provides management solutions
  • Conduct routine farm visits to assess fish health and management practices
  • Collaborates with VPH for fish health certification for trade or export

Large Animal Unit

  • Provides vaccinations for livestock against major infectious diseases e.g. anthrax, FMD
  • Provides deworming and parasite control in cattle, goats, sheep
  • Treats sick livestock for infectious, metabolic and nutritional diseases
  • Performs routine and emergency surgeries such as dehorning, castration
  • Handles obstetrical cases including dystocia and retained placenta
  • Provides post-surgical and post-partum care to ensure full recovery

Equine Unit

  • Provides treatment for a wide range of diseases in horses and donkeys
  • Manages emergency conditions such as colic, lameness or injuries
  • Carries out vaccination and deworming programs against common equine diseases/parasites
  • Conducts surgical procedures in horses/donkeys
  • Offers rehabilitation programs for injured horses

Avian Unit

  • Provides vaccinations against major poultry diseases e.g. Newcastle, Gumboro, fowl pox, Mareks
  • Provides deworming and parasite control in poultry and exotic birds
  • Treats both infectious and nutritional diseases in poultry and other birds
  • Provides on-farm consultation and treatment services for poultry farms
  • Uses necropsy (post-mortem) to identify cause of disease and death
  • Provides emergency care for injured or sick birds
  • Offers beak trimming, claw trimming and wing clipping when necessary
  • Monitors growth rate, feed conversion, and productivity in poultry flock

Wildlife & Micro-Livestock

  • Provide veterinary oversight for zoos, wildlife parks, sanctuaries, conservation centres and micro-livestock
  • Implement surveillance for zoonotic diseases at human–wildlife interfaces
  • Support biodiversity and wildlife welfare programs in collaboration with the Ministry of Environment
  • Monitor and approve wildlife and exotic animal translocations or exhibitions
  • Maintain a database of wildlife facilities, apiaries, and licensed handlers

Porcine Unit

  • Provide diagnosis and treatment of infectious and non-infectious diseases of pigs
  • Provide deworming, parasite control and vaccination services against major pig diseases
  • Provide on-farm consultation and treatment services for pig farms
